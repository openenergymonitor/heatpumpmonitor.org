Design-parameter SPF predictor

Seasonal performance from the design sheet alone: the weather-compensation curve implied by heat loss and emitter sizing, priced over a standard weather year with a load-dependent ideal-Carnot model (H*). No monitoring data required. Defaults are calibrated to the median observed system in the HeatpumpMonitor.org fleet: annual heat demand, hot-water share and the coldest-day flow temperature all match the fleet medians.

Weather-compensation curve

Heat-weighted mean flow temperature the emitters require at each outside temperature — the curve the controls should be commissioned to. It sits below the design point because internal and solar gains carry part of the load; the design anchor assumes no gains.

Predicted COP by outside temperature

η × ideal Carnot at effective condenser/evaporator temperatures, including the cycling floor at low load and the defrost derating — visible as the dip in the frosting band around 0 °C. The curve runs off-scale in mild weather, where the lift is tiny but almost no heat is delivered — the seasonal harmonic mean weighs it accordingly.

Model: H*_design — heat-weighted harmonic mean of an ideal Carnot COP with load-dependent condenser/evaporator offsets, computed over the design weather-compensation curve and a standard weather year (Llanberis 2024, half-hourly temperature and solar, shifted so the year's 0.4th-percentile cold matches the design outside temperature). Space heating demand nets off internal gains (body heat, lighting & appliances) and solar gains (irradiance × aperture × 0.9), smoothed over 24 h to represent thermal mass, clipped at zero. Defrost is a COP derating in the frosting band (−2…+2 °C, fading by +6 °C), calibrated to fleet and field measurements (~0.15–0.2 SPF/yr); H* itself stays defrost-free. Radiator exponent 1.3. Validated against a dynamic simulation fleet to ±0.2 SPF (90%) with truthful inputs; see docs/08_design_parameter_model.md. Assumes constant room setpoint, open-loop hydraulics and weather compensation commissioned to actual demand.
